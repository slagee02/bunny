The Basics to Getting a New Bunny!

Steps if You're Looking to Buy a Rabbit

  1. Research and visit your local animal shelter or contact a breeder to find the bunny of your dreams
  2. Spend some time with the bunny, bunnies have such different personalities it’s great to find the one you’re most compatible with!
  3. Get to Shopping! You need to create the best possible environment for your bunny (more details below)
  4. Bunny-proof your house. It’s important for your bunnies to have some time outside of the cage so you need to make sure that can occur safely.

Items Checklist

  • A very large cage/another large habitat.
    • Rabbits need enough space to comfortably hop about and play! A quick google search can tell you the size cage you need and where to find one
    • Don’t rely on the starter kit pet shops may provide, the cages tend to be too small and rabbits should only reside there for a short period of time.
  • Unlimited hay and a hay holder
  • Ceramic food bowl, plastic water bottle, and food pellets
    • A water dish can be used but is not recommended as they can get knocked over or dirty easily.
  • A Hideaway
    • Your bunny would love a place to hide away in and feel completely secure.
  • Litterbox and pellets (or some other bunny-safe litter material)
  • Toys!
  • Grooming supplies
    • Brush/comb
    • Nail clippers
  • bedding

Litterbox Training

It’s honestly not that hard to potty train a rabbit, as they are quite intelligent animals. I was a little nervous when I got my bunny since he was the only rabbit who wasn’t using the litterbox. I was very surprised at how quickly he learned to start using it. There were only a few steps I took to get him to learn as well. Firstly, when he would poop outside of the litterbox, I would put it in the litterbox so that he realizes that’s where he should let nature take its course! Secondly I put his hay right by his litterbox because he loves to eat it when he’s going, so he became more comfortable using the litterbox. Lastly, I would reward him when he used the litterbox. In no time I had a pretty trained rabbit! If your rabbit is having trouble, no worries they’ll pick it up eventually (they can be stubborn sometimes)!
