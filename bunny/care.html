Caring For Your Very First Rabbit

Caring for a bunny is honestly pretty easy, and an interesting experience because it teaches you routine, and the responsibility needed to not only take care of a bunny, but also yourself. Something funny I’ve noticed is that since I’ve been making sure my rabbit eats well, I’ve been eating well too! I wake up early enough to feed him, I check on him a lot, I play with him when he’s most active, and I make him a nice little dinner of veggies! Another essential part of care is cleaning! A rabbit’s cage tends to get a little messy, especially if they are not litter box trained. I spot-clean my rabbit's cage 1-2 times a day and fully clean the whole habitat every 1-2 weeks depending on how messy he gets. Rabbits like to be neat but when they get excited, they make little messes sometimes. It is also important to groom your rabbit too and keep its nails at a reasonable length (for their and your benefit!).

There are three main areas to focus on when it comes to caring for a rabbit. The first one would be basic care which deals with maintaining its habitat and basic needs such as food, water, hay, and other supplies needed to make sure your bunny is doing its best. The next thing to focus on is the diet so that you can promote your rabbit’s health. It is also essential to make sure your rabbit is getting exercise and has play time so that it can be happy!
